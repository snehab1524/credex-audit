# Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or CTO at a 5–50 person SaaS startup, Series A or below, who directly approves software tool purchases. They're paying $500–$5,000/month across AI tools and haven't reviewed that spend since the initial purchase. They're not CFOs (don't care about unit economics deeply) but they are budget-conscious and would act immediately on a clear, specific savings recommendation.

**Secondary:** Solo founder / indie developer running a small team, paying retail for Cursor + Claude and not aware they could be on a cheaper plan.

## What They Google Before Wanting This Tool

- "cursor pro vs business plan"
- "github copilot vs cursor 2025"
- "is chatgpt team worth it"
- "claude team plan vs pro"
- "ai tools cost startup"
- "reduce SaaS spend startup"

These are intent-rich searches — someone already questioning if they're overpaying.

## Where They Hang Out Online

- **r/SaaS**, **r/startups**, **r/ExperiencedDevs** — engineering spend discussions come up monthly
- **Hacker News** — Show HN posts with "free tool" framing get traction with technical founders
- **Indie Hackers** — Heavy overlap with solo devs paying for AI tools
- **Slack: Online Geniuses, Ramen Profitable, Indie Worldwide** — founder Slack groups where tool recommendations spread organically
- **X (Twitter)** — #buildinpublic, developer Twitter, accounts like @levelsio, @tdinh_me whose audiences are indie devs
- **Dev.to and Hashnode** — Written tutorials rank fast and have engaged developer audiences

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1:**
- Post a Show HN: "I built a free tool that audits your AI tool subscriptions" — minimal pitch, link directly to the tool. HN drives 200–500 visits from a decent Show HN.
- Share in 3 relevant Slack groups: Indie Worldwide, Ramen Profitable, one local startup community.

**Week 2:**
- Write a short data post: "We audited 50 AI stacks. Here's what we found." (use real early data). Post to Dev.to. Share in relevant subreddits — r/SaaS, r/devops.
- DM 20 founders on X who have recently posted about Cursor or AI tool costs. Personalized message: "Built this for exactly your situation — takes 2 min."

**Week 3:**
- Find 3 podcasters or newsletter writers in the "indie hacker / founder tools" space. Offer a guest data angle: "AI tool overspend patterns we're seeing." No pitch, just interesting data.
- Post the most surprising finding from early audits as a Twitter thread. Include shareable audit examples (anonymized).

**Week 4:**
- Activate referral loop: anyone who completes an audit sees a "Share this audit" prompt. The shareable URL with OG preview is the viral loop.
- Reach out to Hacker News community accounts or newsletters (e.g., TLDR Dev) with data angle.

## Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with companies using excess AI credits. Each of those companies has engineering managers — exactly our target user. A personal intro from a Credex advisor saying "we built this free audit tool, try it before our next call" has a conversion rate no paid channel can match.
